How it started

Robert Lucchi is the son of the late Mario Lucchi and Catherine Ravioli Lucchi. His parents immigrated from Bologna, Italy to settle into the Cabrini Avenue section of West Scranton in the early 1900’s. It was here that Robert learned the art of wine making by helping his father at a very young age.

Mark Lucchi and his father Robert, are vintners of the old-fashioned homemade wine school. Robert planted the original vines in the 1970’s, which today produces multiple barrels of their Concord Estate wine. Mark watched his father trek to the family’s home basement to make wine, which became the family's annual tradition. Little did they know at the time their greatest passion would lead them down the road of discover, bonding and now sharing their hard work and labor to the public.

From our vineyard on Perry Avenue to your glass.

Lucchi Family Wine Cellars is a family-run winery in northeastern Pennsylvania. Robert and Mark Lucchi are the vintners of the old-fashioned, homemade wine school. The grapes that Lucchi Family Wine Cellars use are either home-grown or locally bought. The original vines were planted in the 1970’s on the Lucchi family property in Scranton, Pennsylvania, and today, the grapes harvested from these vines produce multiple barrels of Lucchi Family Wine Cellar’s Concord Estate wine.

Quality control

Mary Lucchi, Mark’s mother and Robert’s wife, is the tastemeister. So, when the men in her life create their wine, it is Mrs. Lucchi’s duty to raise her glass in approval or send the duo back to the vineyard to start anew.
